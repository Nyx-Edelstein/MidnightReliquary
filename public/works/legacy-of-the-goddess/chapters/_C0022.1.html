The wagon rocked violently, then knifed to the side and veered off the path. Link heard the shattering of wood as a wheel broke. He closed his eyes and covered his ears, humming to himself to drown out the noise.

It’s not real. It’s not real. It’s not…

He opened his eyes. He was standing on a cobbled path in an open field beside a wagon with a broken wheel. His father banged at the dowels with a hammer as he worked swiftly to replace it. A younger version of himself stood off to the side, crying as his stepmother chided him for his folly.

“Not this,” Link said to himself. “Anything but this.”

A shadow passed overhead, and a cold chill ran down his spine. The kargarok’s shrill cry sounded out over the field. Link tore his eyes away from the scene as he sped along the path. Urik and Ryn were still there, just ahead. He wasn’t lost yet, and he was not going to be taken in. He ran, not looking back, ignoring the terrified shrieks of his stepmother and shutting out the memory of his father forcing him into the iron chest. He closed his eyes as he passed a weeping child on the path, holding onto the broken and bloodied remains of his parents. “You did this!” the child accused him as he ran. “You did this! It’s all your fault! Come back here and face your mistake!”

Link caught up with Ryn and Urik just as they passed under an archway. Ryn looked to him, worried.

“I’m okay,” Link said. “It’s okay. It’s behind me now. It’s all behind me.”

“You’re worrying me, Link,” Ryn said. “You sure you’re okay? You look like you’ve seen a ghost.”

“Heh,” Link said, doing his best to stay calm. His heart pounded in his chest. He looked around, bewildered. They were… standing in a courtyard?

“You looked, didn’t you?” Ryn groaned. “I ficking told you not to look.”

“I tried, okay?” Link said. “I think it’s harder for me than it is for you. I have… a lot of bad memories.”

“What, and you think I don’t?” Ryn said reproachfully.

“No,” Link said, rubbing his neck. “That’s not what I meant.”

“Whatever,” Ryn said. “It’s not important.”

“Uh… where’s Urik?” Link asked, noticing that they were alone in the courtyard.

“Urik?” Ryn said, looking confused. “What are you talking about? Who’s Urik?”

Oh, no.

All the color drained from Link’s face. He stared at Ryn in horror as he backed away. This was—

Ryn snorted, then doubled over with laughter. “Relax, I’m just ficking with you,” she said, wiping away a tear. “Urik’s gone to get Ibboso. He said he’d right back.”

“…That’s not funny, Ryn,” Link said bitterly.

“Says you,” she said. “You should’ve seen your face!”

“How can you laugh like that?” Link said in dismay. “This is serious!”

“Don’t you get it?” Ryn said. “The only power they have over you is what you give them. See, that’s the difference between us. You dwell on your darkness. I just laugh at mine and tell it to go fick itself.”

Link rubbed his face. Maybe she was right.

A deep voice called to them from afar: “So. You are the boy Lucia speaks so fondly of?”

Link started, but was careful to keep his eyes on his feet. “Uh, Ryn?”

“Yep, that one’s real,” she said hastily. “Pretty sure that’s him.”

“You’re sure?”

“Uh huh.”

Link looked up. A tall man with braided black hair and light tan skin approached them from the other end of the courtyard. He was clad in regal armor in the ancient Gerudo style—red, black, and gold. A crown made of bone sat upon his head. There was no mistaking it. This was the man from his vision.

“Ibboso,” Link said under his breath.

The man stopped roughly six paces away. “My, my. And who is this fair maiden you’ve brought with you?”

Ryn snorted. “Flattered, but you’re not my type.”

“Watch your tongue with me, you insolent whore,” Ibboso said.

Link stepped between them. Ibboso caught his eye. Link hesitated.

“Mighty courageous of you to walk so brazenly into my domain,” Ibboso said. He raised his sword to level at Link’s neck. A shard of gold gleamed in its hilt. “And I see you’ve brought a gift! How kind of you to bring it back to me.”

Link’s hand went to his necklace. To his horror, it wasn’t there. He looked up to see Ibboso holding it by the chain. The ancient Gerudo king stopped to admire the fragment of Courage before tucking it into his belt.

“Stop!” Urik said from a dark corridor just ahead. “He’s Lightsworn, and chosen of the Goddesses! Hylia sent him. Please—”

“—I SAID BE SILENT, WITCH!” Ibboso roared. A cold wind blew through the courtyard and two heavy wooden doors slammed shut, trapping Urik inside.

Ibboso turned back to them and laughed. “You think yourself a hero? No… you are only the pale imitation of one. I know your kind all too well. You haven’t the strength to stand before the darkness.”

“Link, don’t listen—”

Ryn cut off abruptly. Link shivered as the hair on the back of his neck stood up. He glanced back at her and his breath caught. There she stood, petrified in mid-speech, a soft red glow around her body.

No…

“Your woman isn’t going to save you, boy,” Ibboso said. “Now pick up your sword.”

Link jumped and spun as he heard the clang of metal on stone. At his feet lay his sword and shield.

“Ibboso…” Link began. “I’m not here to fight you. I just want to help. We can talk this through. Whatever you want, we don’t have to—”

“—Enough!” The man’s eyes narrowed as a snarl of pure rage danced across his face. His eyes burned like cinders plucked from a dying campfire.

Link stood his ground. A tense silence hung in the air.

“Words,” Ibboso said. “Empty words, from an empty boy. You are no Lightsworn. You are little more than a coward, playacting the hero. I have awaited this opportunity for ages, and I will not be denied my prize.”

Something nagged at the back of Link’s mind. He glanced back over his shoulder, out of the courtyard and towards the town below. “…You’re in pain, aren’t you?” he said. “You just want this to be over. I saw it in my vision. You and your tribe—”

“—You know nothing!” Ibboso said. “Do not speak about that which you do not know! Now, cease your childish prattling and pick up your weapon! You need not fear. I promise to end you swiftly.”

“Ibboso, please—”

“—I said pick it up!” Ibboso bellowed, dropping into a fighting stance. “You are weak, and I will take your place!”

“No!” Link yelled. “It doesn’t have to be like this!”

“Choose your next words carefully, boy,” Ibboso spat. “They will be your last.”
