Excerpt from Avalian Governance: A Treatise
(Updated 35th of First Brisk, 547 CE)

While the monarch holds ultimate authority in all matters, the Council exists to advise the Crown and represent the concerns of both the Noble Houses and the common people. The monarch’s authority is delegated as follows:

The Queen’s Law has the duty of maintaining the written law; determining when a crime has been committed, who is responsible, and to what degree; and adjudicating disagreements between private citizens. They have the power to delegate any and all of these responsibilities to magistrates throughout the kingdom, who ultimately answer to the Queen’s Law. They may also administer court in the absence of the monarch. The position is currently held by Chancellor Nyllara Zan.

The Queen’s Justice has the duty of enforcing the law and upholding public order; prosecuting potential criminals and arguing their guilt before the law; and determining the punishments for breaking the law. They delegate this power to various administrative groups throughout the kingdom, and may also administer court in the absence of the monarch. The position is currently held by Chancellor Adresin Lotan.

The Lord General is the penultimate authority over the military. They administer the outposts, command the armed forces, and oversee logistics. Their role on the council is to request and provision goods and services for military use, and to ensure the monarch and the council members are aware of military concerns. The position is currently held by Wymar Letholdus.

The House of Nobles consists of representatives from each of the twenty Noble Houses. While representatives may attend council meetings to voice their house’s interests before the Crown, attendance is not compulsory. Each year, they elect a Head from among themselves to attend all meetings year-round. Countess Lensa Oda currently holds this position.

The House of Commons consists of twelve influential citizens elected by the common people to represent their interests. Every two years, those seeking a position may petition the Queen’s Law for candidacy. Twelve candidates are then chosen by moot—a gathering where citizens of Castletown voice their support for their preferred representatives. The Queen’s Justice oversees and validates the results. The Head of the House of Commons oversees tax collection, manages the kingdom’s financial affairs, and advocates for the citizenry before the Crown and the courts. Prime Counselor Taetus currently holds this position.

Xayla’s Journal

1st of Last Brisk, 548 CE:

And so begins a new year. Let us hope our good fortune holds, and that the Blight is ended swiftly.

So much to do. Just taking a moment to jot down my thoughts…

During the attack, Arlinkar suffered a host of critical, life-threatening wounds—organ damage, several shattered bones, the works. I healed the worst of it and kept him from dying (also cured his bloodrage affliction—praise the Goddesses). He still spent several days bedridden while the Healing took effect, and was only able to walk around with a cane the day before my coronation. Because of the frankly insane amount of magic I used while empowered, his recovery has been spectacular. He should be in fighting shape in a little over a week—assuming he eats enough and doesn’t overexert himself. I want to get him working as soon as possible, but we can’t risk breaking the Healing effect. I don’t know how well he’s holding up mentally. Haven’t asked. He’s hard to read.

My own wounds from the battle were comparatively minor and have already healed, but the magical exhaustion was another matter entirely. I’m past the worst of it now, but even approaching my limit again while recovering could permanently damage my affinity. Given my track record with control, I’d rather not risk it. Full recovery should take another three weeks, assuming I don’t do anything foolish in the meantime.

I’ve massively increased castle security since the assassination attempt. No one enters or leaves without official business and an armed escort. It’s been a challenge—the number of petitioners has skyrocketed, as expected. I’ve ordered the watchtowers manned around the clock, all entrances under constant surveillance, and routine patrols with continuous communication between posts. Hopefully it’ll be enough to deter further attacks until we can root out any remaining enemy agents.

Everything is on fire, and I have no idea what to do about it. The loss of Jarrensvale, the ongoing war effort, and the sheer death toll—estimated at 30% of the total population—has dealt a massive blow to our infrastructure. Everything south of the floodplains and east of the Avali River has been abandoned, at least until the Blight ends. If each region weren’t mostly self-sufficient, this would be completely insurmountable. As it stands, holding things together in the coming months is going to be incredibly difficult.

The influx of refugees from the eastern lands and the floodplains is putting a heavy strain on Castletown and the Royal Province. We’ve granted the Noble Houses significant tax breaks and issued bonds in exchange for housing and feeding refugees, but they’re at their limit. There’s scarcely enough food to go around, and even with heavy rationing, this arrangement isn’t sustainable—not in the middle of Brisk when food production is minimal and supplies are limited. If we don’t find a solution before supplies run out, the city could face revolt. My counselors estimate we have 3-4 months before that happens.

The crime rate has surged dramatically despite enforcement crackdowns—most of it economically motivated. Assault and petty theft are the worst offenses. The streets are decidedly unsafe, with constant threats of assault and other violent crimes. Those who can defend themselves or afford armed guards manage well enough, but everyone else faces considerable danger.

There were isolated incidents of rioting and pillaging in the days immediately following the attack. Many of the offenders were rounded up and publicly executed at Wymar’s command—grim and unfortunate, but the action served as a clear message and effective deterrent. Hopefully that will remain the case when he leaves for the front.

Banditry is also on the rise outside the city. Leaderless bands of Blin are still ravaging the countryside, adding to the ever-present threat of fiend encroachment. As a consequence, caravan traffic has taken a hit and it has become that much more expensive to travel and transport goods. Nominally, forces have been dispatched to counter this, but I fear it won’t be enough. Still, I don’t have any ideas, and until Talus has been reclaimed and the hordes repelled, the main concern right now is the war effort. Larger towns will likely hold out, but smaller villages will continue to sustain losses and the death toll will continue to rise until the bands have been eliminated. Undoubtedly, this will have cascading effects on long-term production and stability.

As for reconstruction and the war effort, I’ve left those matters entirely to the Council and Wymar respectively. They seem to be managing well enough without me mucking things up.

I should probably make some public appearances, but fear of assassins has me paralyzed. All the more reason to hope for Ari’s speedy recovery. I’m considering a couple public speeches and a few private meetings with the nobility—in the safety of the castle, of course. But I know much more needs to be done on this front, and I’m not sure where to begin.

A fair number among the populace seem to believe I’m capable of much more than I actually am—that I’m simply indifferent to their plight. I’m not sure how much I should say about my limitations. I’d really rather not address it at all, but…

Xayla buried her face in her hands, slouching back at her desk. Even in the rare moments of peace and solitude she managed to find lately, she couldn’t escape the constant work and stress. She had been second-guessing herself endlessly. Every idea she had, she turned over and over in her mind, reconsidering from every angle. She was terrified—terrified of doing the wrong thing, of failing to do the right thing, or worst of all, of inadvertently interfering with whatever plans Avalia had set in motion and making everything worse.

She wasn’t even sure it was safe to write as much in her own diary.

On some level, she knew it was silly. How could she be so vain as to believe herself capable of derailing plans set in motion by the Holy Mother herself? Hadn’t Avalia trusted her enough to appoint her as her personal champion? Logically, she knew that doing anything was better than doing nothing. Yet somehow, that only made it worse.

She knew she couldn’t keep this up for much longer. Too much was expected of her. She had to act.

She rubbed her face and pulled the next parchment from the seemingly endless pile. Reports of a rogue squadron of mages who had fled during the initial assault on the city. For those blessed by the Goddesses to abandon their duty was the gravest of offenses, both against the Crown and the Faith. Normally, hunting them down would fall to the Veilwyrd. But given the Veilwyrd’s current state…

Xayla thought back to Wymar’s request. She had approved it without thinking—how daft of her! They couldn’t afford to waste the Veilwyrd on the front lines like that. There was far too much that needed doing elsewhere.

She hoped it wasn’t too late. She set the parchment aside and resolved to speak with Wymar again at the first opportunity.

Deep breath.

Oh, how she wished she had someone to confide in—someone she could rely on. Null was proving less than useless more often than not. Anli was missing. And Nira…

Xayla hoped Ari might eventually fill that role in some capacity. He could never replace Nira… but with someone as competent as him watching her back, she could at least feel safe leaving the castle. Still, it would be a few more days before he was fully recovered, and he wasn’t exactly in the best place himself right now.

There was Wymar. He was family, and she trusted him with her life. He’d always been open and honest with her, and she respected him deeply. But he was already shouldering the entire war effort—she couldn’t justify burdening him further unless she had no other choice.

She supposed Lensa had always been a positive force in her life, for better or worse. Lensa had been Xayla’s tutor since she was a young girl, and even now acted as a de-facto aide and guide in matters of high society. The poor woman had been saddled with her when Father gave up knowing what to do with his wayward daughter. She had always been stern and… well, unforgiving. But Xayla trusted her. Perhaps she would have some insight as to how she should proceed in her new role?

Then there was Counselor Taetus. For all his faults, even Xayla had to admit he was good at his job. There was a reason Father had respected him, after all.

Hmm. Perhaps…
