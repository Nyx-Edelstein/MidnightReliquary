This fragment? The Holy Mother’s intervention? Zelda and Fi, and latest of all, the Lightsworn artifact? It was too much to be pure coincidence.

Every day after learning of the initial attack, he prayed to Hylia for an opportunity to make a difference. He had gotten that and more. So, so much more. He had saved over a thousand lives that fateful day. He stared down a Warlord, and had his prayers literally answered—even if it didn’t happen as he would have expected it to. He was knighted, became the Queen’s Champion—who was herself Hylia’s Champion—and was bestowed with the mark of the Holy Mother herself. And merely a single day after that, they stumbled upon a magical artifact that had laid dormant for quite possibly thousands of years, and just so happened to be in the company of the singular entity for which its engraved message was intended, who then followed the instructions to use said artifact in an ancient ritual to cause Link to awaken as a Lightsworn.

Fate. It had to be. Hylia had to have planned this, somehow, long before she sacrificed herself. Somehow, she knew all of this ahead of time.

Was he a true Lightsworn yet? He wasn’t sure. But at the same time, he was gifted with a series of visions—mysterious locations that he’d never seen or even heard spoken of before. The Goddesses themselves may as well have written a message in the stars: “Link: Go here!”

And one of them? One of them had to be hidden away in the mountains, surrounded by a desert. And where was he presently headed? To meet with the Lynx, who was hidden away in the mountains, surrounded by a desert. The Lynx, who was in turn the one individual who seemed to have any information whatsoever about their enemy.

He wasn’t crazy. There was absolutely no way any sane and rational person could experience these events and not believe there was a guiding hand at work. He was clearly meant to have some important part to play in this story.

Dare he think it? Dare he, the son of a middling merchant-lord, believe himself destined to join the ranks of the heroes of legend? Of surpassing every story he had ever heard told, and facing down the same dark force that had killed Hylia?

Dear Goddess, he had prayed that fateful day. Holy Mother, guide my blade. I will be your champion.

He wasn’t sure what his part in all this would be, but it certainly wasn’t a small one, acting as a glorified doormat while someone else sat around and talked all day. He clutched the golden fragment in his hand—

—and nearly fell out of the wagon as it came to an abrupt halt. Wait, what? Why were they stopping? It should be at least a couple hours before it was time to make camp.

Fearing the worst, Link grabbed his sword and hopped down out of the wagon, boots hitting the sandy gravel with a soft thud. He spotted Koja riding past on horseback.

“What’s going on?” he called out. “Why’d we stop?”

“Don’t know!” she yelled, continuing to ride forward. She turned her head as she passed. “A messenger from Last Bastion is on the way. The Queen ordered us to stop.”

That was… unexpected. Unexpected, and worrying.

Link steeled himself, getting ready to deal with some more fate.

-o-o-o-

Around her, the world spun. She heard the words almost before they were uttered. Somewhere in the back of her mind… some part of her in tune with the fragments… she already knew the words to be true.

The Zora’s Domain has fallen. There are refugees. More by the hour. They are desperate and in need of aid. They say it is the work of Ja’bu. Your Grace, no one knows what to do! There are so many of them…

It was the dream. The same dream. Except, this time it was no dream. This was simply the last of the pieces clicking into place; a puzzle she had seen many times from many angles, but only just now fully understood.

But… no… she had… she had told the Zora about her dreams! And Golvara… Golvara had…

Your Grace!

“Your Grace! Are you alright?”

Zelda snapped to as an arm jostled her out of it. She felt empty, like all the life had been drained from her body. A cold chill crawled up her spine as she looked around. There she stood, facing the messenger from Last Bastion as a crowd gathered around them.

And the River Hylia was running backwards.
