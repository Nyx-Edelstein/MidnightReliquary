Xayla’s Research Notes - The Sanctern

The Sanctern is undoubtedly the most powerful artifact in existence, though surprisingly little is known about its true nature. Vestele tells me it was created by the Celestial Sovereigns themselves, and left to Avalia after their work was complete. The Lorekeepers claims it to be the ultimate source of all magic. It is theorized to take the following forms:

1. As a unified whole which—supposedly—would grant the wielder the full power of the Celestial Triad, and thus the ability to recreate the world according to their heart’s desire.

2. As three “Pieces”: Mind, Will, and Spirit.

3. As “Fragments” or “Shards”: Each piece can be further divided into nine fragments. These magnify their wielder’s power or grant lesser abilities related to the three whole pieces.

Supposedly each piece or fragment can only be wielded by individuals attuned to their essence. They also have arcane rules governing their possession, and have been known to “seek out” their owners. I do not know precisely what any of this means nor what such entails (and Vestele assures me I am not alone in that), though it does explain certain peculiarities both myself and Ari have witnessed.

Mind:

The embodiment of Caelon’s powers. Grants cosmic powers of divination. Legends say it allows its wielder to see plainly into the future. It was the first piece to be fragmented and lost to time—the event occurred before the era of the Old Kingdom. When fragmented, each shard grants lesser abilities related to divination:

1. Ability to scry the location of closely bonded individuals.

2. Sometimes grants prophetic dreams, often highly symbolic or metaphorical and open to interpretation.

3. Ability to communicate telepathically with closely bonded individuals.

4. Ability to scry at any recently visited location.

5. Prophetic dreams become more clearly defined.

6-9: ???

Will:

The embodiment of Vaka’s powers. Grants tremendous strength and endurance, and significantly heightens magical affinity. Also fragmented well before current historical record, with countless wars fought over possession of its fragments. Each fragment considerably boosts the wielder’s physical and magical prowess.

With some number of fragments, supposedly the wielder gains access to an ability similar to “Stasis” (i.e. - The Basilisk’s Gaze). Nothing further is known at present. It is hypothesized that several fragments (or perhaps the full piece) may confer abilities related to Siphon and Transformation.

Spirit:

The embodiment of Nenore’s powers. This is the least known, even though a whole piece was supposedly wielded as recently as the time of the Hero’s Stand. The fragments are said to grant “luck,” which manifests as preternatural reflexes and instincts that border on premonition.

Two halves of Nothing. Equal and opposite; a paradox and its own resolution; able to exist only because their sum total was still, precisely, Nothing.

My mind awoke.

I drifted in a sea of stars, without focus or bearing. Before me stretched an expanse I somehow recognized as the whole of the cosmos, reaching from infinity at one end and receding into infinity at the other.

Within that expanse, the three layers of reality stood juxtaposed: Divinity, Concept, and Substance. Each a reflection of the others, each flowing and twisting in complex fractal shapes at the borders. I gazed upon the center where all things met, and with a start, I realized something there gazed back at me.

Ageless eons passed in a single ephemeral moment, and I saw how all things were connected, how all things were, and how all manner of things could come to be. In that moment, I knew eternity. I was understanding, and I was perception. I became knowledge. I became wisdom.

Above me, two brilliant red flames ignited. Entranced, I watched the fires intensify and grow rapidly at a frightening pace. Swirling, they rushed toward me—faster and faster, hotter and brighter, until their radiance outshone all else. I tried to brace myself against the coming shockwave, but before I could react, I was torn apart and engulfed by a heat hotter than any sun.

The whole of my being burst into a raging inferno, wreathed in black flames with a white-hot sphere of condensed fury at its center. Eternal and all-consuming, I burned with raw, crackling energy—Will concentrated to its densest substance. I became one with that fire, and it was one with me. It energized me and drove me to seek perfection. This fire was strength, and it was unity. It was passion and desire; ardor and determination. Raw. Overwhelming. Power.

The flames abated, and an off-white crystalline structure materialized around me. Bones and sinew interwove intricately, a biological scaffolding of bewildering complexity. Muscle fibers stitched together, organs and regulatory systems grew and connected inside, and layers of delicate tan skin folded around it all. My vision retreated to two conical sections emanating from a central locus. The ethereal plane of the divine fell out of my perception just as the plane of concept became sharper and more focused. I felt a pulsing in my chest and in my ears, and knew that I was alive.

This was a sensation unlike any other. Overwhelmed with emotion, I laughed, then cried out in shock. With this new form came a mind, and in that mind vast streams of knowledge bent and eddied with the current of my thoughts. But I had no time to reflect, as the world around me continued to shift. Euphoria and wonder swelled inside my chest as a beautiful green-blue sphere rose from below, expanding to encompass my view.

Only then did I realize I was falling, and felt a pang of terror. But just as quickly the fear subsided, replaced by a serenity bequeathed by assurances whispered on the winds. Of course I could not be harmed. This realm and its physical laws were little more than humble suggestions next to my own divine will. Emboldened by Spirit, I threw myself at the sky and flew.

The wind screamed in my ears as I streaked through the clouds. Below, rivers crisscrossed into lakes and estuaries before meeting with a vast ocean expanding in all directions. I watched mountains jut out, defiantly presenting their peaks to the sky. Verdant hills came into focus, rising and undulating across the landscape. And with a deafening crack, I slammed into the ground, screaming my elation to the heavens for all the world to hear.

Rising from the small crater left by my descent, my feet brushed lightly against the dew-wet grass. I delighted to feel the softness of its blades between my toes and the coarse earth beneath my feet. With a raised hand I met the sun’s resplendent caress, just as a cool breeze whipped about, tossing my hair and bringing with it scents of charred earth and stone.

Belatedly, the realization struck: I was not alone. I turned sharply to see three majestic feminine silhouettes towering above me. An instant of recognition, and I fell to my knees in reverence of their divine glory. I knew they had been with me this whole time. They’d molded me, shaped me from the Void itself. They were my mothers, my creators, my progenitors.

Caelon, Vaka, and Nenore.
