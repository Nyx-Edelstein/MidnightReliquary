The team watched as her avatar appeared nearby and glided away towards one of the Lizalfos. The Lizalfos immediately panicked, attacked and dispersed her avatar, screeched out a warning, then fled. The others scattered.

“Erm—I did not expect that,” Link said, stunned. “Okay, yeah. We should—”

“I feel strange,” Koja said, “Really tired all of a sudden. I think I… I think…” She scratched her neck, stumbled, then fainted in the middle of the party.

“Poison dart!” Jedal exclaimed as he examined her. He removed the quill from her skin and picked her up in a soldier’s carry.

“Retreat! Let’s get out of here!” Izu commanded.

Muttering a curse under his breath, Link drew his sword and donned his shield. He took up one flank as the party formed up and began to flee—

He jumped as he heard a strange popping sound, followed by a hissing noise as a thick, acrid dust cloud enveloped them. The odor was completely overpowering, and he choked on the fumes. His vision swam and he began to feel woozy.

No! He couldn’t… he wouldn’t…

He shook his head vigorously and dashed ahead, trying desperately to fight it, to escape the…

-o-

Link came to his senses and found himself tied to a stake, surrounded by a throng of Lizalfos in the courtyard of the compound. The rest of the party was restrained with him.

Alright. Don’t panic, he thought to himself. It was not looking good, but at least they were alive. It wasn’t over yet.

The shaman finished his address to the crowd and began activating the lift. Link struggled against his binds, but it was no use. He attempted to call out to the others, but they were still unconscious.

The lift sunk down into the abyss, and the sunlight gradually faded away, leaving them in shadow. A few seconds later, a dim reddish light, like the color of a twilight sky, lit up the platform with an eerie, unnatural glow. As they descended, the intensity of the light increased until he could clearly see the surroundings.

The air was thick with surreal motes of black fog, and Link found it difficult to see through it. The cavern they found themselves in was enormous, far larger than anything he could have imagined. He couldn’t even make out the ceiling above. And as they reached the ground below, he saw that the area around the platform was covered in bones and scraps of equipment, all lit with that strange red hue that appeared to emanate from everywhere at once.

Fick.

The shaman said something to the enforcers. Link tried to communicate with Fi to get her to translate, but her avatar was instantly attacked and destroyed.

A pair of enforcers lifted Link’s stake and hurled him off the platform onto the ground, then the others one by one. They were all still wearing their armor—thank the Goddesses—but their weapons were missing. His too, he noted. He still had the stone tablet strapped to his side, but his sword and shield were nowhere to be found.

The lift activated and rose back up, leaving them alone in this vile place. Link struggled again, but his binds held fast. A distant roar echoed against the walls of the cavern, sending shivers down his spine. Then again. Slightly louder. Closer.

Link’s heart pounded in his chest. His magic ignited in his veins as he strained in futility against his bindings, desperate to break free. His mind raced as he tried to come up with a plan, but he couldn’t—

Behind and to his left he heard a pained cry that shifted into a low growl. Gazra!

“Over here!” Link called to him.

The sound of snapping ropes, and a moment later Link was free. He stretched and flexed his aching wrists, then immediately set to work helping unbind the others. None of them were lucid yet, but Koja and Izu were stirring.

The ground shook. Slightly at first, but it grew in intensity with each passing breath.

He was working to free Kellin now. Once he had her loose, he slapped her, hard. She immediately came to, eyes darting around in a panic.

“Quickly!” he said, motioning to the others. “There’s no time!”

Groggily, she set to work helping him and Grenja rouse the rest.

A deafening roar shook the cavern, and Link wheeled about. The monster that emerged from the darkness in the distance was unlike anything he had seen before. A towering lizard-like abomination with jagged spikes running down its back, its eyes glowing red with malice, and its long, misshapen maw filled with rows and rows of spiny teeth. Molten rock seemed to flow along its spine under its scales, and its massive, clawed forearms looked like they could mangle an oxgoat with ease.

Link wilted under the gaze of Kith’raxis—the dread god of the Lizalfos—then steeled himself for an impossible fight. He glanced back at the team. At least everyone was on their feet. But they were all weaponless, groggy from poison, and with no room to maneuver.

It wasn’t looking good. If any of them survived this…
