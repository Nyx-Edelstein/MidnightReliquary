Vigilant and wary, Link proceeded down the dark, winding corridor. As he rounded the second corner, he saw light up ahead. Further in, the corridor opened to a large, dimly-lit room. The light wasn’t quite enough to see by, but he could at least make out a few details.

Just ahead, at the eastern side of the room, was a roped-off area with four posts in a square. He couldn’t be certain, but it looked very much like a sparring arena. Link strapped his lantern to his belt and put on his shield—just in case. Who knew what to expect in this place?

He scanned the room.

If he had to guess, it was somewhere between a half and a third the size of the cavern with the remains of the massive worm-thing. The ceiling however was just as high, and diffuse light shone through from a circular opening at its center. Below the opening, at the center of the room, was a massive pit that ran lengthwise across the room, east to west. It was a good six or seven meters deep, and was filled with sand at the bottom. Half-a-dozen or so iron ladders lined the eastern and western edges of the pit at regular intervals, and at the northern and southern sides were rows of staggered benches.

…Strange. Very strange. Was it a fighting pit? Link wasn’t sure what else it could be.

He decided to explore the room, walking clockwise along the pit.

Along the southern wall he found a short recess blocked off by thick iron bars. At the far side—about three meters in—was an ornate chest trimmed in green and gold.

Hmm…

He got a closer look. The bars were thick and sturdy—no way he was breaking through, even with magic. Maybe with a long enough pole he could get at the chest?

He continued on for now. At the western end of the room was a row of target dummies and a couple of weapon racks. The weapons they held looked to be in decent condition, though all of the edges were blunted and the points rounded off.

Link spun, taking in the room as a whole and seeing it for the first time. Was the Obsidian Spire just an abandoned Lightsworn training ground?

Relaxing a measure, he continued on clockwise around the pit. Past the northern row of stone benches, at the northeast corner of the room, was a metal hatch facing diagonally upward. It had two handles on either side, and looked to open horizontally inwards. A sign hung over it, but Link couldn’t read what it said. It didn’t look like any language he was familiar with.

Just off to the side were two switches that looked just like the one from earlier. Above them in a line were three exposed gears resting on spokes that jutted out a couple inches from the wall.

“Fi, can you read that sign?”

Negative, Master. However, based on previous observations at the Temple of Time, I conjecture it may be Old Script; probability: seventy point six percent. Conditional on the language not being Old Script, my models overwhelmingly predict it to be a close variant.

Old Script, huh? Link took a few moments to dig out some paper and a charcoal pen out of his pack, copying down the writing for later. If nothing else, perhaps Vestele or someone else at the temple could translate it.

Finished, he stowed everything away. Now, back for that chest…

On his way back past the weapons racks, Link took one of the spears and the halberd and crudely tied them together using a length of rope. Heading back to the southern edge of the room, he poked his makeshift chest-opening implement through the iron bars.

…Urg!

It was significantly more unwieldy than he expected when holding it like this. The crude implement wobbled uncontrollably at the far end, and despite his best efforts he was unable to open the chest from this distance. He also tried snagging the outside hook of the halberd to the corner of the chest and pulling it towards him, but it was either too heavy to budge or was otherwise fixed to the ground.

A few more minutes of unfruitful struggle and Link decided to give up. There had to be some other way in.

Unsure what else to do, he threw aside the makeshift implement and headed back over to the sparring arena. Steeling himself, he readied his sword and shield and hopped inside.

…He waited with bated breath, but nothing happened.

Link felt like smacking himself. What was he expecting? Some random opponent to just pop out of the ground and attack him? Right now he felt rather foolish, but mere moments ago it had made some kind of sense…

He shook his head and climbed out of the arena, returning to the switches and the hatch at the northwest corner. Maybe they held the key to progressing?

Link inspected the hatch more closely. The handles were latched shut with a simple locking mechanism. He flipped it open and pushed inward. The flaps gave way without so much as a creak, and Link peered inside.

On the other side was a dark, narrow tunnel leading away into darkness towards the north. The path was blocked by thick iron bars, and the rounded walls and ceiling were incredibly smooth. Directly ahead and to his right—to the east and south—were thick metal grates. The holes in the grates were less than an inch in diameter. Link couldn’t make out anything on the other side of either grate.

There was nothing else of note here.

Hmm.

Link drew back and closed the hatch, eyeing the switches. He was hesitant to fool with them, but it didn’t seem he had much choice.

Left or right?

He chose the right switch and gave it a firm pull. It wouldn’t budge.

Okay. Left then. He gave it a tug. Click click click click click! It ratcheted back into place as he released it, and the gears above spun. Link heard a low rumbling noise coming from nowhere in particular. He turned around to see guardrails rise up around the pit.

Also, a stone door now blocked the entrance to the room from whence he came.

Nope. He was not having any of that.

Link pulled the right switch. It ratcheted back into place with several clicks, and the gears spun in the opposite direction. A moment later and a loud howling sound erupted from the center of the room, drowning out everything else.

The central pit was now a miniature sandstorm.

“Aaaah!” Link remarked, grabbing the left switch again. It wouldn’t budge. He definitely didn’t panic as he tried the right one a second time.

The guardrails lowered and the entrance was once again open.

Phew. He wiped his brow and let out a sigh of relief.

The bars were still there.

The right switch, activating the sandstorm in the pit?

Nope.

Hmm…

That certainly seemed worth checking out. But first, Link needed to make sure there weren’t any other surprises down here.

Exploring the rest of the pit, he found several small indentations along the floor, each less than two feet deep. There were six in total; each of them uniquely shaped—each the bottom half of one of the perfect solids: sphere, tetrahedron, cube, octahedron, dodecahedron, icosahedron.

Was it a puzzle? If so, Link had no idea what he was supposed to do to solve it.

Reasonably confident there weren’t any hidden traps or lurking monsters, he approached the skeletons and—

Stalfos weren’t real—they were just stories. Scary tales told to children to get them to behave. They weren’t real.

The remnants of their tattered clothing was likewise uninteresting. He did however find a red gemstone on a necklace hanging down into one of the skeleton’s rib cages.

Blue gemstone: blue-runed door. Red gemstone…? Was it really that easy?

Ah-ha!

Urg.

CRACK!

Click! Fuzz.

The green block vanished. It just… vanished, in a shower of green light.

Link scratched his head. What did that do? He headed back down the corridor, checking each of the rooms and halls in turn. Nope. Nothing there. Nothing had changed, except—

Except the green block was now back exactly where it started. The rabbit chittered and squeaked loudly in the next room over as if to mock him.

-o-

Link opted for a different approach.

Slowly, calmly, he approached the rabbit, slinking down to make himself look less threatening.

“Hey there,” Link said softly. “I don’t want to hurt you. I just want the gem in the chest on your back. Can I have the gem? Please?”

“Squeak.”

Uhh… Link wasn’t sure what that meant.

“I’ll trade you for it?”

“Squeak.”

Link fished out a bag of shiny pebbles from his pack, then took a step forward. The rabbit’s ear twitched and it crouched like it was about to hop away. Link stopped.

“Was that a yes?”

“Squeak squeak.”

Link waited for a good minute before extending the bag and—

—The rabbit turned and hopped off.

-o-

Link took aim, carefully lining up the shot—

Clink! It harmlessly glanced off the rabbit’s side.

“SQUEAK!”

…Was he just imagining it, or did the rabbit sound offended?

It hopped off into the next room.

-o-

“Gonna… gonna getcha… this time…” Link said between gasps.

He had it now. He was going to push the block down into the hallway and just in front of the metal plate. Then the little bugger would be cornered when he ran that way, and then Link could finally catch it, take the gemstone, and move on from this forsaken place.

It was all coming together. Just a little further.

The cheeky bastard scampered past him, just out of reach. Link paid it no mind. It was about to get what it had coming.

“Just wait, you little shit,” he muttered, pausing for a short breather. “I have just the plan for— wait, where are you going? No… nonono—”

Click! Fuzz.

Link fell forward onto his hands and knees as the block dematerialized. He punched the ground over and over as inarticulate cries of anguish escaped him.

-o-

It was done. It was finally over!

Link cackled like a madman as he held the blue gemstone high in the air above his head. He then slid down to the ground with his back against the green block to catch his breath.

His eyes fell on the pile of rabbit-rubble near the corner of the room.

“Hey, don’t you look at me like that. You brought this on yourself,” he said accusingly, pointing a finger at the debris. “You have no one to blame but yourself! It didn’t have to end like this, but you made me! I was happy to be friends, but you forced me to… you…”

What in damnation was he saying? Link smacked his forehead, snapping out of it. He needed to get out of here, and fast. He needed to get some fresh air before he lost it.

-o-

Ragged and weary, Link drug himself up another flight of stairs. He neared the top and—

The stairs closed up behind him.

The stairs…

Seriously?! How many more trials did he have to go through? Wasn’t the rabbit enough??

He pressed ahead with weapons ready. It wasn’t like there was anywhere else to go.

Link looked ahead into an open, dimly-lit room, pausing at the doorway to get an idea of what horrors he was about to face. He rubbed his eyes. No; he wasn’t seeing things—the room was filled with large bones. Gargantuan, oversized bones that looked like they belonged to a creature from a storybook.

He was really starting to hate bones.

At the center of the room, sitting inside a pentagonal-shaped recess was… a shiny, semi-circular object? Link couldn’t quite make it out from this distance, but one end glowed with a faint green light.

Steeling himself, he stepped towards—

Shink!

Predictably, as soon as he set foot into the room, the stone door slid shut behind him. Runes along the walls flared, and an aperture in the ceiling opened. A massive plume of smoke flowed down into the bones.

Link took a step back, pressing up against the wall.

The bones stirred, rumbled, and swirled about, snapping into place.

Goddess Above.

In front of him stood a towering skeletal abomination. A… skeletal Hinox? A Stalnox!? The abomination swiveled its head left and right, and its sunken, shadowy eyes fixated on Link.

It lurched forward.

-o-

Link half-stumbled into base camp from the west, trailing puddles of water. Koja sat by herself on the stairs, tending to her nails. Ryn was lying sprawled out on a bedroll, staring up at the ceiling. Grenja was doing pushups near the entrance.

Ryn rose to her feet as she noticed him. “Uh, Link…? Why are you soaking wet?” She sounded concerned.

“Long story,” Link said, wringing water out of his tunic. “I’m fine. Food first. Fi, do the thing.”

“Yes, Master.” Fi twirled and danced overhead as ribbons of chromatic vapor settled around him. Link snatched up a piece of jerky and tore into it as he felt his vigor renewed. Mmmmmm. So good.

“Link?” Ryn repeated. “Why are you wet?”

“Mmph.” Link chewed and swallowed. “Because I slid down an aqueduct and landed in a pool of water inside some kind of lounge area just over there.” He pointed back to the living quarters.

“…Why?”

“To evade a horde of angry Stalchildren.” Link gave up chewing and swallowed the rest whole, sipping some water to help it down.

“What the fick, man?” Grenja darted over. “Stal-children?” He paced around, lifting Link’s arm. “Did they bite you? Are you infected?!”

He swatted Grenja away. “No, I’m just— Hey! Stop that.”

“Sorry.”

“Link—why were you being chased by a horde of angry Stalchildren?” Ryn pressed.

“I dunno,” Link said plainly. “I ran into them because I was looking for the last block to solve the puzzle.”

“The what?”

“There’s this puzzle in the sandpit room with a bunch of block-shaped holes at the bottom—I think I mentioned that, right? Well anyway, the blocks that fit into the holes are spread all throughout the structure. Now that I have the transfabrulator, I’ve gotta go all over the place and retrace my steps through the trials so I can take the blocks back there and solve the puzzle.”

“…Blocks? Trans—what?” Ryn shook her head in confusion. “Link, slow down. What’s a trans… fabricator?”

Link unhooked the metalic half-circle device from his belt. It fit comfortably in his palm. He held it up for them to inspect, flat side facing outward. The runes on its surface glowed faintly.

“Well I’ll be,” Grenja said slowly. He whistled, giving Link a soft pat on the back. “I can’t believe you actually found it! Nice going, man. I always knew you had it in you.”

“What?” Koja said, scurrying over. “What’d he find?”

“I’ll, uh, let you tell her, Link,” Grenja said. “You earned it.”

Link looked to Koja. She rolled her eyes and shook her head, waving a hand.

“Fi calls it a transfabrulator,” Link said. “Whatever that means. Bit of a mouthful if you ask me.”

“Some kind of ancient Lightsworn artifact, I take it?” Ryn asked.

Link shrugged. “Yeah, probably.”

“Looks neat,” Koja said, bending over to get a good look. “What’s it do?”

Link smiled with a glint in his eye. “Just watch,” he said. On the side of the device, he selected the cube from the six options corresponding to the six perfect solids. He aimed away from the group and squeezed the handle. A perfectly-shaped green cube materialized where he pointed.

Koja gaped at him wordlessly, doing a double take.

“Wow,” Grenja said. “Er—never thought I’d see it up close.”

“Uh-huh,” Ryn said dubiously.

“That’s not all,” Link said with a grin. He flipped a toggle-switch near his thumb and pointed off at a wall in the distance. He gave the device a firm squeeze and the cube went flying towards the wall with incredible speed.

CRASH! Clunk-tink!

It hit the wall with absurd force, causing everyone to flinch as a veritable thunderclap reverberated through the hall. Link had tested it—the blocks the device made were every bit as heavy as the one in… that place.

He checked back with his companions. The three of them stood in wordless awe. Link tapped the front of the device and the block fuzzed, warping away with a shower of light.

“I want one,” Grenja said, breaking the silence.

“Yeah, bro. Like there’s going to be a whole pile of them just lying around,” Koja said sarcastically.

“Just the one, unfortunately,” Link affirmed. “I mean, as far as I know.”

“Uh, are you sure you should be slamming it into the walls like that?” Grenja asked.

“…No,” Link said with a weak smile, rubbing his neck. “But I checked—the walls seem pretty solid and it didn’t even leave a scratch.”

“Still…” Koja said uneasily.

“Yeah, I’ll keep the potential catastrophes to a minimum,” Link said. “Promise.”

“Where did you find it, anyway?” Ryn asked.

-o-

“—Wait, was this before or after the rabbit?”

“I do not want to talk about the rabbit,” Link said, trying and failing to keep the tension out of his voice.

“Okay, okay!” Ryn said with a laugh, holding her hands up. “Sorry, won’t mention it again. So did you get that block you were after?”

Link shook his head. “Nope, but I found this.” He put away the transfabrulator and took out a weighty green gemstone from a pouch at his side. “I’m not sure where the matching door is yet, and I still have go back for the block. Just thought I’d stop by and rest up before trying again. Speaking of—”

Link took in a deep breath and stretched, bouncing on the balls of his feet to get his blood pumping. “I should probably get going if I want to make any more progress before I need to sleep,” he said. He waved to Ryn, turning to go.

“Don’t take too long,” Ryn said, flopping back down on her bedroll. “At this rate I’m going to die of boredom. …If Koja doesn’t kill me first.”

“Wait—hold up,” Koja called out from behind. “Remind me, why’d you need the block again?”

Link stopped and pivoted. “To solve the puzzle?” he answered. “So I can get whatever’s in that chest—the one in the sandpit room.”

He had every expectation the chest just held another gemstone, but there was no reason to pass it up. It would probably be required later on.

“Uh-huh.” Koja said flatly. “The chest behind the iron bars, right?”

“…Yes?” Link answered uncertainly.

“The chest behind the iron bars with an ominous sandstorm pit nearby, containing a puzzle that is almost certainly a trap?” Koja clarified.

“Yep, that’d be the one,” Link said, his patience slipping.

“And have you thought to avoid the trap by just breaking through the bars?”

“Yeah, of course,” Link said. “That was the first…” He trailed off as his thoughts caught up to him. He glanced down to the transfabrulator on his belt.

“…Oh.”
