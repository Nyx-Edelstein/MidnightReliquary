The assassins advanced.

No, Xayla thought, panic rising.

Avalia—Goddess Above! Please, don’t let it end like this!

“Eep!”

She couldn’t what? She spun around, looking up and to her left.

A window.

Huh.

Alright, that wasn’t going to work.

Wait. She was overthinking this.

No! Xayla drew back as the man convulsed, his pained moans breaking into ragged gasps. Three days?!

Wymar clasped his hands and leaned back slightly. “At first, with you leading the charge, they broke completely. A total rout. We swept the field clean—it was like something straight out of a folktale.”

Xayla hesitated. “I… see.” That unsettled her. What had happened while she was unconscious?

“Wymar? What aren’t you saying?"

Wymar grew stiff. He didn’t answer.

Wymar met her eyes, his expression grave. “I’m so sorry, Princess. I know how close you were. She fell defending your father.”

The world collapsed around her. Xayla sank back into her bed, distant and numb.

“You can take solace in knowing she died a hero,” Wymar said from somewhere far away.

“Thank you, Wymar,” Xayla said quietly. “Please leave me.”

In a world that was not hers, a door swung closed.

Xayla stood before the gathered masses in the castle’s Sanctum, dressed in regal splendor. The High Bishop was giving a speech—something about faith and unity in dark times. She couldn’t focus. Who was “the Lynx” that the messenger mentioned? Where did Vesyrn go with her stolen fragments of Mind, and who had them now? Who was this “Exiled King” that sent the assassins after her?

She’d always wanted to be a strong ruler like Father. Now she was Queen, and felt duty-bound to guide her kingdom through this darkness toward a better future. Yet with so much to do and so many unanswered questions, where should she even begin?

Xayla looked around at her empty study, pondering.

“Null? Are you there?” she asked.

The ethereal specter had not appeared since that day. Xayla wondered if she ever would again.

Three knocks sounded at the door below. The sound echoed through the study, and Xayla felt a pang of loss. “He’s here, Your Grace,” a guardsman announced.

“Thank you. Send him in,” she called.

Xayla placed a marker in her book and rose, making her way down the stairs. She poured herself a cup of wine at the table below, and another for her guest. Speaking of…

The man sauntered up to her uneasily, casting furtive glances in every direction. He walked with a cane, and his chest was heavily bandaged. Even with magic, it would be several days before he was fully healed. She would have to see what she could do to make him more comfortable.

He took her hand and bowed his head. “Just ‘Ari,’ if you don’t mind, Your Grace.”

“Ari it is,” Xayla said, gesturing to the table. “Please, sit. We have much to discuss.”
