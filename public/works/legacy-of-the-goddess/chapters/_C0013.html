The shadows on the surface of the shimmering barrier around her seemed to be alive. Scales of shadow and flame twisted and turned, flowing over one another as a gigantic serpent slithered about.

Lying in wait. Biding its time.

The fire burned brightly; brighter and hotter than anything she imagined possible. Zelda tried to turn and cover her eyes, but it was no use. She could not hide from the beautiful, awesome, terrible flame. It was everywhere at once—she even felt it burning within her very being. From where she stood, protected as she was, it was dulled and diluted to almost nothing; an echo, far and distant. But it was there, nonetheless. Unmistakable. Pure, yet complex.

It was the warmth of true love, and also the cold emptiness it left behind with distance and time. It was the passion of lovers in the throes of ecstasy, and also the twisting fear of inadequacy and rejection. It was the desire to reach for the forbidden, and also the humiliating shame of guilt and denial. It was the grief of those touched by death, and the bittersweet memories of those they lost. It was the urge to create marvelous wonders and to explore the unknown, and also fields of crumbling tombstones and the ruins of kingdoms long forgotten.

The light of the flame shifted in her mind, refracting into colors; red, orange, gold, and then black, where the flames met and joined with the scales of the serpent. A black so dark and deep it drank in the light around it—and yet it burned still: deep, angry, and violent. Zelda knew in an instant she was nothing next to this being. Were it not for the shimmering barrier that was the Goddesses’ protection, the tiniest mote of those black flames would consume her and her entire world as surely as the sun would rise.

This thing…

This was Hylia’s Demise.

The writing mass was a face. A face consumed by the fire, eyes alight with its dark, wondrous radiance.

And the face was laughing.
