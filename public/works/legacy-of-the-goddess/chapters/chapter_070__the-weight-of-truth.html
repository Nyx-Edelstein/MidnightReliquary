Xayla clutched her ears, gasping for breath. Just as she regained her bearings, Ari dove in beside her with Vestele at his side. The priestess curled up behind the wooden platform for cover.

“What’s happening?!” Xayla shouted.

“Stay down!” Ari commanded.

“Your Grace! Come with me! We have to get you—”

“A bomb! He has another bomb!”

Before she could react, a pair of arms had her by the shoulders and she was dragged into a nearby building. She dove forward and tensed up, expecting another explosion. None came.

“Xayla! Are you alright?" Ari, calling to her from the doorway.

“I—I think so,” she managed, her voice shaky. “Hit my head. Some cuts and bruises. What’s happening out there?”

“Stay here,” Ari said as three guardsmen poured into the building. He pointed to Cove. “You! Keep her safe!” he barked, then ran off.

Xayla pushed herself up against the wall. One of the guardsmen offered her a flask, which she used to rinse away the dirt and blood.

She had been such a fool. Such a fool, to think this would end well.
